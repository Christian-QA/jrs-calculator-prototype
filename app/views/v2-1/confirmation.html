Breakdown of calculations

You told us your employee gets paid a variable amount each time and has worked for you for more than 12 months. There are 2 methods we can use to work out pay. We’ve used the one that gave the higher figure. The furlough grant is 80% of this.

Method 1: we’ve taken the amounts they were paid for equivalent pay periods in 2019 and applied this to the number of furlough days in each pay period.

Method 2: we’ve worked out their average daily earnings in the last tax year, by dividing their total pay by the number of calendar days between 6 April 2019 and the day before furlough (or 5 April 2020 if later). Then we’ve multiplied that by the number of furlough days in each pay period.

For pay period 8 July to 15 July 2020

Calculate pay based on furlough days

Method 1: take pay from same dates last year.

Last year’s dates overlap two pay periods so in period one:

  1. Start with £550.77 (from pay day 9 July last year).
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  3. Multiply by 2 (furlough days).

Total for period one = £157.14

Then in period two:

  1. Start with £600 (from pay day 14 July last year).
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  3. Multiply by 5 (furlough days).

Total for period two = £428.57

Then:

  1. Add £157.14 + £428.57 (the totals from both pay periods).

Total pay from same dates last year = £585.71

Method 2: take the total pay from employee start date (or 6 April 2019, if earlier) to day before the employee's furlough start date (or 5 April 2020, if later):

  1. Start with £26000 (total pay).
  2. Divide by 333 (days employed).
  3. Multiply by 5 (furlough days).

Total average pay last year = £546.46

Then take the higher figure from methods 1 and 2:

  • £585.71 (method 1: pay from same dates last year).
  • £546.46 (method 2: average pay last year).

Total pay based on furlough days = £585.71

Furlough grant

  1. Take £585.71 (pay based on furlough days).
  2. Multiply by 80%.

Calculated furlough grant = £468.57

This exceeds the maximum furlough grant for this pay period, which is:

  • 16 furlough days in March at £80.65 + 15 furlough days in July at £83.34 = £456.92.

Therefore we use the maximum furlough grant amount.

Total furlough grant for pay period = £456.92

Employer National Insurance contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Add £200 (employer-funded pay).
  3. Add £77.99 (employer top up).
  4. Add £320 (additional pay).
  5. Round down to the nearest pound.
  6. Deduct £166 (NICs threshold for 20/21 tax year).
  7. Multiply by 13.8%.
  8. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  9. Multiply by 7 (furlough days).
  10. Divide by £546.56 (pay based on furlough pay plus top up).
  11. Multiply by £468.57 (furlough grant).

Calculated NIC grant = £44.96

This exceeds the maximum National Insurance contribution grant for this pay period, which is:

  • £468.57 furlough grant x 13.8% = £43.97.

Therefore we use the maximum National Insurance contribution grant amount.

Total NI grant for pay period = £43.97

Employer minimum pension contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Round down to the nearest pound.
  3. Deduct £67.43 (£118 Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings for 20/21 tax year, divide by 7 days in pay period and multiply by 4 furlough days).
  4. Multiply by 3%.
Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
