Detailed breakdown of calculations

You told us this employee gets paid a regular amount each time. We’ve worked out their daily earnings and multiplied by the number of furlough days in each pay period. The furlough grant is 80% of this.

There’s a maximum amount you can claim for furlough grant and Employer National Insurance contributions. If this affects your claim, we’ve adjusted the calculations.

Calculations are rounded to the nearest penny unless otherwise stated.

For pay period 8 April to 15 April 2020

Calculate pay based on furlough days

Take the pay in pay period:

  1. Start with £585.71 (from pay period).
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  3. Multiply by 7 (furlough days).

Total pay based on furlough days = £585.71

Furlough grant

  1. Take £585.71 (pay based on furlough days).
  2. Multiply by 80%.
Total furlough grant for pay period = £456.92

Employer National Insurance contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Round down to the nearest pound.
  3. Deduct £166 (NICs threshold for 20/21 tax year).
  4. Multiply by 13.8%.
  5. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  6. Multiply by 7 (furlough days).
Total NI grant for pay period = £44.96

Employer minimum pension contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Round down to the nearest pound.
  3. Deduct £118 (Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings for 20/21 tax year).
  4. Multiply by 3%.
Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
