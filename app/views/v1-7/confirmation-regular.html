Detailed breakdown of calculations

To calculate the furlough grant - Your employee gets paid a regular amount each time, so we apportion the amount based on the amount of furlough days in the pay period. We take the relevant pay figure and multiply it by 80% and then check its not higher than the furlough cap.

To calculate the NIC grant - Take furlough grant and add any employer contribution to any top up and any additional pay and then multiply by 13.8%. Check that is not higher than the NIC Cap.

To calculate the Pension grant - Take furlough grant and deduct Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings then multiply by 3%.

For pay period 8 April to 15 April 2020

Furlough grant

Take the pay in pay period:

  1. Start with £550 (from pay period).
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period).
  3. Multiply by 7 (days in matching pay period).
  4. Multiply by 80%.
  5. Which is £468.57 (calculated furlough grant) .

Then compare this against the maximum grant and use the lower of:

  • £468.57 (calculated furlough grant).
  • £456.92 (maximum furlough grant = 16 furlough days in March at £80.65 + 15 furlough days in April at £83.34).
Total furlough grant for pay period = £456.92
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer National Insurance contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Add £200 (employer-funded pay).
  3. Add £77.99 (employer top up).
  4. Add £320 (additional pay).
  5. Which is £546 (rounded down to nearest pound) .
  6. Deduct £166 (NICs threshold for 20/21 tax year).
  7. Multiply by 13.8%.
  8. Divide by 7 (days in the pay period).
  9. Multiply by 7 (furlough days in pay period).
  10. Divide by £546.56 (total pay in pay period).
  11. Multiply by £468.57 (furlough grant).
  12. Which is £44.96

Then compare this against the maximum NIC grant for this pay period and use the lower of:

  • £44.96 (calculated NIC grant).
  • £45.97 (maximum NIC grant = £468.57 furlough grant x 13.8%).
Total NI grant for pay period = £44.96
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer minimum pension contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant).
  2. Which is £468 (rounded down to nearest pound).
  3. Deduct £67.43 (£118 Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings divided by 4 furlough days).
  4. Multiply by 3%.
Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.
