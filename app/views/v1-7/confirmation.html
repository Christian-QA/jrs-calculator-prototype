Detailed breakdown of calculations

Furlough grant

For pay period 1 March to 31 March 2020

Choose the higher of either:

  • 80% of £2400.30 (same month’s wages from the previous year)
  • 80% of £2380.10 (average monthly wages for the 2019 to 2020 tax year)

which is 80% of £2400.30 = £1920.24

Then:

  1. Divide by 31 days in the pay period
  2. Multiply by 11 furlough days in the pay period
  3. Multiply by 80%

Total furlough grant for pay period = £681.38

For pay period 1 April to 30 April 2020

Choose the higher of either:

  • 80% of £2439.30 (same month’s wages from the previous year)
  • 80% of £2380.10 (average monthly wages for the 2019 to 2020 tax year)

which is 80% of £2439.30 = £1951.44

Then:

  1. Divide by 30 days in the pay period
  2. Multiply by 30 furlough days in the pay period
  3. Multiply by 80%

Total furlough grant for pay period = £1951.44

Total furlough grant for claim

£681.38 + £1951.44 = £2632.82

Employer National Insurance contributions

For pay period 1 March to 31 March 2020

  1. Employer-funded pay £1548.58
  2. Add £400 employer top up
  3. Add £320 additional pay
  4. Add £681.38 furlough grant
  5. Deduct £719 NICs threshold
  6. Multiply by 13.8%
  7. Divide by 31 days in the pay period
  8. Multiply by 11 furlough days in the pay period
  9. Divide by £2400.30 total pay for furlough days in the pay period
  10. Multiply by £681.38 furlough grant

Total NI grant for pay period = £45.50

For pay period 1 April to 30 April 2020

  1. Employer-funded pay £1548.58
  2. Add £681.38 furlough grant
  3. Deduct £719 NICs threshold
  4. Multiply by 13.8%
  5. Divide by 30 days in the pay period
  6. Multiply by 30 furlough days in the pay period
  7. Divide by £2400.30 total pay for furlough days in the pay period
  8. Multiply by £681.38 furlough grant

Total NI grant for pay period = £45.50

Total NI grant for claim

£45.50 + £45.50 = £91.00

Employer minimum pension contributions

For pay period 1 March to 31 March 2020

  1. Furlough grant £2632.82
  2. Deduct £520 (this is the Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings)
  3. Multiply by 3%

Total pension grant for pay period = £45.50

For pay period 1 April to 30 April 2020

  1. Furlough grant £681.38
  2. Deduct £520 (this is the Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings)
  3. Multiply by 3%

Total pension grant for pay period = £45.50

Total pension grant for claim

£45.50 + £45.50 = £91.00
