Detailed breakdown of calculations

For pay period 8 April to 15 April 2020

Calculate relevant pay amount

Take pay from same dates last year.

Last year’s dates overlap two pay periods:

Start with £550 from pay period 3 April to 9 April 2019.
Divide by 7 days in pay period.
Multiply by 2 days in matching pay period.

Total for period = £157.14

Then:

Start with £600 from pay period 10 April to 16 April 2019.
Divide by 7 days in pay period.
Multiply by 5 days in matching pay period.

Total for period = £428.57

Then:

Add the totals, £157.14 + £428.57.
Total pay from same dates last year = £585.71

Then:

Take the average pay from employee start date (or 6 April 2019, if earlier) to day before the employee's furlough start date (or 5 April 2020, if earlier).

Start with £26000 total pay
Divide by 333 days employed
Multiply by 7 days in matching pay period

Average pay for last year = £546.46

Compare these figures and use the higher of:

£585.71 (pay from same dates last year)
£546.46 (average pay for last year)

which = £585.71

As this figure exceeds the maximum grant, use the lower of:

£585.71 (pay from same dates last year)
£576.92 (maximum grant for pay period)
Work out the maximum grant you can claim
(opens in a new window or tab)

Therefore relevant pay amount = £576.92

Calculate furlough grant

Start with £576.92 relevant pay amount.
Multiply by 80%.

Total furlough grant for pay period = £468.57
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer National Insurance contributions

Start with £468.57 furlough grant.
Add £200 employer-funded pay.
Add £77.99 employer top up.
Add £320 additional pay.
Round down to nearest pound = £546.
Deduct £166 NICs threshold for 20/21 tax year.
Multiply by 13.8%.
Divide by 7 days in the pay period.
Multiply by 7 furlough days in pay period.
Divide by £546.56 total pay in pay period.
Multiply by £468.57 furlough grant.

Total NI grant for pay period = £44.96
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer minimum pension contributions

Start with £468.57 furlough grant.
Round down to nearest pound = £468.
Deduct £67.43 (this is the Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings, £118, apportioned to 4 days in furlough period ).
Multiply by 3%

Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.
