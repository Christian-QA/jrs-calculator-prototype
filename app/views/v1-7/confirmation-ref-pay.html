Detailed breakdown of calculations

For pay period 8 April to 15 April 2020

Furlough grant

Take pay from same dates last year. Last year’s dates overlap two pay periods.

So in period one:

  1. Start with £550 (from pay day 9 April last year)
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period)
  3. Multiply by 2 (days in matching pay period)
  4. Which is £157.14

Then in period two:

  1. Start with £600 (from pay day 14 April last year)
  2. Divide by 7 (days in pay period)
  3. Multiply by 5 (days in matching pay period)
  4. Which is £428.57

Then:

  1. Add £157.14 + £428.57 (the totals from both pay periods)
  2. Which is £585.71 (total pay from same dates last year)

Then take the total pay from employee start date (or 6 April 2019, if earlier) to day before the employee's furlough start date (or 5 April 2020, if earlier).

  1. Start with £26000 (total pay)
  2. Divide by 333 (days employed)
  3. Multiply by 7 (days in matching pay period)
  4. Which is £546.46 (average pay for last year)

Then compare these figures and use the higher of:

  1. £585.71 (pay from same dates last year)
  2. £546.46 (average pay for last year)

As this figure exceeds the maximum grant, use the lower of:

  1. £585.71 (pay from same dates last year)
  2. £576.92 (maximum grant for pay period)
    Work out the maximum grant you can claim
    (opens in a new window or tab)
  3. Multiply by 80%
Total furlough grant for pay period = £468.57
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer National Insurance contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant)
  2. Add £200 (employer-funded pay)
  3. Add £77.99 (employer top up)
  4. Add £320 (additional pay)
  5. Which is £546 (rounded down to nearest pound)
  6. Deduct £166 (NICs threshold for 20/21 tax year)
  7. Multiply by 13.8%
  8. Divide by 7 (days in the pay period)
  9. Multiply by 7 (furlough days in pay period)
  10. Divide by £546.56 (total pay in pay period)
  11. Multiply by £468.57 (furlough grant)
Total NI grant for pay period = £44.96
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.

Employer minimum pension contributions

  1. Start with £468.57 (furlough grant)
  2. Which is £468 (rounded down to nearest pound)
  3. Deduct £67.43 (this is the Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings, £118, apportioned to 4 days in furlough period )
  4. Multiply by 3%
Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
The total is rounded to the nearest penny.
