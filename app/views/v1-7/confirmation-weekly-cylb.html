Detailed breakdown of calculations

For pay period 8 April to 15 April 2020

Furlough grant

Same week’s wages from the previous overlaps two pay periods:

  1. Pay period 3 April to 9 April 2019 = £550
  2. Divide by 7 days in week
  3. Multiply by 2 days in matching pay period
  4. Total for period = £157.14
  1. Pay period 10 April to 16 April 2019= £600
  2. Divide by 7 days in week
  3. Multiply by 5 days in matching pay period
  4. Total for period = £428.57

Same week’s wages from the previous year:
£157.14 + £428.57 = £585.71

Choose the higher of either:

  • 80% of £585.71
  • 80% of £546.46 (average week's wages for the 2019 to 2020 tax year)

which is 80% of £585.71 = £468.57

Then:

  1. Divide by 7 days in the pay period
  2. Multiply by 7 furlough days in the pay period
  3. Multiply by 80%

Total furlough grant for pay period = £468.57

Employer National Insurance contributions

  1. Add £77.99 employer top up
  2. Add £468.57 furlough grant
  3. Deduct £166 NICs threshold
  4. Multiply by 13.8%
  5. Divide by 7 days in the pay period
  6. Multiply by 7 furlough days in the pay period
  7. Divide by £546.56 total pay in the pay period
  8. Multiply by £468.57 furlough grant

Total NI grant for pay period = £44.96

Employer minimum pension contributions

  1. Furlough grant £468.57
  2. Deduct £67.43 (this is the Lower Level of Qualifying Earnings, £118, apportioned to 4 days in furlough period )
  3. Multiply by 3%

Total pension grant for pay period = £12.03
